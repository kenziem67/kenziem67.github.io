During our performance, the participants caught on quicker than anticipated.
This meant that the end result was more cohesive than we initially thought it would be. For the origami in the second trial, many stopped trying and this did result in an extremely varied final product as intended.
The element of chance is the choice that you make at the beginning and the result of the choice that you made. How did your origami end up looking? How did you perform your dance? Was there a certain arm you preferred moving first, and where?

The performance is done by the audience. When presented with two choices without context, what will they do?

The audio recorded instructions give them a fixed time to complete every step. There is no going back.


Instructions/script
Macarena
1. Everybody stand up
2. Close your eyes
3. Stick your left hand out
4. Stick your right hand out
5. Turn your hands over one at a time
6. Touch your shoulders one at a time
7. Put your hands on your head one at time
8. Put a hand on each hip one at a time
9. Do a hip circle
10. Jump once

Origami (Cat)
1. Grab a piece of paper and return to your seat
2. Close your eyes
3. Face the paper upwards and move the paper diagonally
4. Fold the paper in half by folding the bottom corner to the top corner
5. Fold in half again by folding the left corner to the right corner
6. Unfold
7. Fold the right corner of the triangle up at an angle
8. Repeat the same step for the left side
9. Fold a portion of the top corner down
10. Open your eyes
